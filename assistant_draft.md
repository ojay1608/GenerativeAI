Healthcare AI and revenue cycle management (RCM) represent a compelling area where technology can reduce friction, improve financial outcomes, and ultimately free up resources for better patient care. The revenue cycle — spanning patient registration, coding, billing, and collections — is rife with manual processes, errors, and delays that cost health systems billions annually. Intelligent automation in this space has the potential to make healthcare administration more accurate, efficient, and equitable.

**Three ways generative AI could transform healthcare RCM:**

- **Automated medical coding and documentation:** Generative AI can analyze clinical notes and automatically suggest accurate ICD and CPT codes, reducing coder workload, minimizing claim denials from coding errors, and accelerating reimbursement timelines.
- **Intelligent claims denial management:** AI can draft and prioritize appeal letters for denied claims by interpreting payer policies, past denial patterns, and clinical context — turning a slow manual process into a faster, more consistent one.
- **Patient-facing billing communication:** Generative AI can produce clear, personalized explanations of medical bills and coverage gaps, helping patients understand their financial responsibilities and reducing confusion that often leads to unpaid balances.
